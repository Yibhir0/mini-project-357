Research Methods

The Survey

This research focuses on understanding the challenges of medication adherence among individuals with chronic diseases and identifying design strategies for a health companion app. By analyzing existing studies, including a quantitative survey conducted in France, the research highlights key user needs, current medication management practices, and the importance of personalized solutions. Addressing these challenges through an intuitive and effective application can improve medication adherence and overall health outcomes. The study reveals that chronic diseases requiring long-term treatment are prevalent, with 40% of individuals over the age of 16 in France affected. Current medication management practices vary, with 43% of patients using a pillbox, indicating a preference for structured tracking tools. However, while 79% claimed to know all their medications, non-adherence rates suggest a gap between knowledge and consistent usage. The study concludes that personalized reminders could significantly improve adherence. Rather than generic alerts, reminders tailored to individual routines and past adherence patterns would be more effective in addressing forgetfulness and ensuring long-term engagement. By integrating insights from this research, the proposed health companion app should focus on adaptive reminders, intuitive medication tracking, and a user-friendly interface. Implementing these features can bridge the gap between awareness and adherence, providing individuals with chronic diseases with a more reliable system for managing their treatments at home.

Personas

The survey results helped create user personas representing different types of users with diverse needs. This approach allowed us to focus on the most common pain points and design solutions accordingly.

User Journey Mapping:

A typical user journey was mapped to understand how users interact with the app and the emotional responses at different stages.

Wireframes

Low-fidelity wireframes were created to visualize the app’s structure before moving into high-fidelity design.

Prototypes

Prototype screenshots serve as a visual bridge between early design concepts and the final user interface. They capture key moments and interactions from the prototype, providing stakeholders and team members with a tangible glimpse into the app's look, feel, and functionality. These snapshots help communicate design intentions clearly, facilitate constructive feedback, and support iterative improvements throughout the development process. By sharing these visuals, teams can align on the user experience and ensure that the design meets both aesthetic and functional goals before moving into more detailed development stages.

Usability Testing

Testing Goals

The usability test was conducted remotely to assess the effectiveness, ease of use, and accessibility of the Health Companion App. The primary objectives included evaluating task completion efficiency, identifying usability issues, and gathering qualitative feedback for improvements.

User Tasks

Participants were assigned specific tasks such as setting up a medication reminder, snoozing reminders, and scheduling a doctor’s appointment to simulate real-world usage. These tasks were designed to test the core features and identify potential usability issues.

Feedback Collection

Feedback was collected through the think-aloud method, structured surveys, and follow-up discussions. Participants shared their thoughts while navigating the app, answering structured questions about ease of use, clarity of instructions, and any challenges faced.

Analysis and Design Iteration

Key findings highlighted areas for improvement, including simplifying the reminder setup process, enhancing accessibility with larger text and higher contrast, and implementing caregiver alerts for dependents. These insights guided iterative design improvements to enhance user experience and engagement.

Reflection

The UX design process played a crucial role in identifying user needs and refining the Health Companion App to create a more intuitive and effective experience. Through user research, usability testing, and iterative design, the process provided valuable insights into the challenges faced by individuals managing chronic health conditions. A key challenge was balancing simplicity with functionality, as older users required a straightforward interface while younger users preferred more customization. This was addressed by implementing a simplified default mode with optional advanced settings. Another challenge was designing for different user groups, such as caregivers who needed a way to monitor medication adherence remotely. This was solved by introducing a caregiver mode. Additionally, some users experienced notification fatigue, which was mitigated by incorporating adaptive reminders that adjusted based on user behavior. Conducting usability testing remotely also posed difficulties in gathering real-time insights, but this was overcome by using screen recordings, structured surveys, and follow-up interviews. Ultimately, the UX design process demonstrated that iterative user feedback is essential in creating a product that effectively meets real user needs, leading to data-driven improvements that enhanced accessibility, engagement, and adherence to medication schedules.
